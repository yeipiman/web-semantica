2) Licensing of the data source and potential license

The datasets provided by Transports Metropolitans de Barcelona (TMB) are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) license.

This license allows copying, redistribution, and adaptation of the data, including for commercial use, as long as proper attribution is given to the original source.

Derived works can also be released under CC BY 4.0 to maintain legal compatibility and encourage data reuse.

3) Resource naming strategy for Metro Interactive Map

The URI design for the metro map application follows this pattern:

Domain:
https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/
(Main namespace for Barcelona transport datasets)

Path:
metro/ - metro map project identifier.

Ontology terms path:
https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/ontology/
(Used for defining classes and properties.
Properties follow camelCase starting with lowercase, e.g., #hasStation, #connectsTo.)

Individuals path:
https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/resource/
(Used for instances of stations, lines, and their relations.)

Naming conventions

  • Classes use PascalCase (e.g., MetroLine, StationLine).
  • Properties use camelCase starting with a lowercase letter (e.g., #hasStation, #isConnectedTo).
  • Instances and data entities use short, readable identifiers in camelCase or lowercase, depending on local conventions.
    Examples:
    https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/resource/metroLine/L1
    https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/resource/station/sagradaFamilia_L2
  • All URIs are persistent and dereferenceable, ensuring long-term accessibility.

Metro-specific examples

  • Metro Line ontology:
    https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/ontology/MetroLine#hasStation
  • Metro Line instance:
    https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/resource/metroLine/L1
  • Station in Line relation:
    https://data.example.org/transport/bcn/metro/resource/station/sagradaFamilia_L2

This naming strategy supports the interactive map features by clearly identifying metro lines, stations, and their relationships, ensuring consistent integration across linked datasets and visualization layers.
