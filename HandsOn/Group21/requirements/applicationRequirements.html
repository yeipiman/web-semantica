Application Requirements — SafeRoad España

1. Summary

The proposed application, SafeRoad España, integrates three open datasets — Traffic Accident Statistics, Population Data, and Fixed Traffic Radars — to analyze and visualize the relationship between accident frequency, population density, and radar presence across Spain. The goal is to provide insights into how traffic radars may contribute to reducing accident rates and improving road safety.

2. Scope

  • In: Visualization of accident density per municipality, radar locations, and population-based normalization (accidents per 10,000 inhabitants); generation of safety indicators and maps.
  • Out: Real-time accident prediction, individual vehicle tracking, or closed data sources.

3. Actors & Use Cases

  • Actor: Researcher, policymaker, or citizen interested in road safety analysis.
  • UC1: Visualize accident density and radar distribution on an interactive map.
  • UC2: Compare accident rates normalized by population for different provinces or municipalities.
  • UC3: Identify municipalities with high accident rates and no nearby radars.
  • UC4: Export aggregated results for further statistical or RDF-based analysis.

4. Functional Requirements (FR)

  1. FR1: Display a national map combining radar locations and accident data.
  2. FR2: Calculate accident rate per capita using population data (accidents per 10,000 inhabitants).
  3. FR3: Compute a Safety Index per municipality, considering accident density, radar coverage, and population.
  4. FR4: Highlight municipalities with above-average accident rates and low radar density.
  5. FR5: Allow exporting results as CSV or provide RDF/SPARQL endpoint access for linked data queries.

5. Non-Functional Requirements (NFR)

  • NFR1: All datasets converted into RDF using vocabularies such as GeoSPARQL, Schema.org, and DCTerms.
  • NFR2: Data hosted and versioned in GitHub; optional SHACL validation of RDF consistency.
  • NFR3: Respect open data license attribution and ensure source citation for each dataset.
  • NFR4: Accessible and responsive web interface for desktop and mobile users.

6. Data Requirements

  • Accidentalidad dataset: Municipality, date, type of accident, fatalities, coordinates.
  • Población dataset: Municipality, total population, year.
  • Radares dataset: Radar ID, coordinates, road section, municipality.
  • Linking strategy: Match datasets by municipality name or INE code; optional geographic proximity for radar–accident links.

8. Acceptance Criteria

  • FR1: When loading the map, all radars and accidents appear with distinct markers or layers.
  • FR2: When filtering by year 2025, only the corresponding accident data are displayed.
  • FR3: When computing the Safety Index, values are normalized (0–100) and shown per municipality.
  • FR4: When exporting data, CSV or RDF files reflect the same aggregated indicators shown in the interface.
