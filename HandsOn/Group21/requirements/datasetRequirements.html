Requirements of the DataBases — SafeRoad España

Datasets Used

  • 2025_Accidentalidad.csv (https://datos.gob.es/es/catalogo/l01379328-accidentalidad-2025-dgt)
  • poblacion_1_enero.csv (https://www.ine.es/jaxiT3/Tabla.htm?t=31304)
  • RADARES_FIJOS_vDTT.csv (https://datos.gob.es/es/catalogo/l01379328-radares-fijos-dgt)

Functional Requirements

  • Accidentalidad 2025 (DGT)
    • R1: The dataset covers road safety and accident data in Spain, including the number of accidents, fatalities, and injuries per municipality or road segment.
    • R2: Data are provided in CSV format and can be easily processed for RDF transformation.
    • R3: The dataset is published under an open license CC BY 4.0 (Attribution 4.0).
    • R4: Each record includes location information (province, municipality, or coordinates) enabling linking with population and radar datasets.
    • R5: Documentation is available on the DGT open data portal, describing variables such as “tipo_accidente”, “número_víctimas”, and “fecha”.
    • R6: The dataset is referenced in other catalogues, including:
      • Accidentes de tráfico — Catálogo datos.gob.es
      • Portal de Datos Abiertos Madrid — catálogo entry
  • Población a 1 de enero (INE)
    • R1: Provides demographic data for Spanish municipalities, including population counts by date, necessary to normalize accident data per capita.
    • R2: Data are downloadable as CSV from the INE website.
    • R3: The dataset is distributed under the CC BY 4.0 open license.
    • R4: The dataset includes municipality codes and names that can be linked to accident and radar datasets via standardized INE codes.
    • R5: Official documentation and metadata are available on the INE portal.
    • R6: The dataset is referenced in multiple national and European open data catalogues:
      • Cifras Oficiales de Población municipal - datos.gob.es
      • Población por distrito y barrio - catálogo entry
  • Radares Fijos DGT
    • R1: The dataset contains information on fixed-speed cameras installed by the DGT across Spain, contributing to road safety.
    • R2: Data are provided in CSV format, with columns for coordinates, road name, and radar type.
    • R3: Published under an open license CC BY 4.0.
    • R4: Each radar includes geographic coordinates (latitude/longitude), allowing linking to accident records and population data via municipality or spatial proximity.
    • R5: Metadata and documentation are available in the DGT open data portal.
    • R6: Dataset appears in additional catalogues, such as:
      • Radares fijos DGT - NAP DGT
      • Radares - datos.gob.es
      • Radares - catálogo entry
