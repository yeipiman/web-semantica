1. Purpose

The application will allow citizens and researchers to explore the trees located in parks and green areas of Madrid. It helps to understand biodiversity, identify green spaces, and support environmental policies.

2. Users & Use Cases

  • Citizen — wants to know which tree species exist in their neighborhood park.
  • Researcher — studies biodiversity in Madrid and compares tree species across districts.
  • City planner — identifies parks with fewer tree varieties to improve urban greening.

3. Functional Requirements

  • FR1: Display all trees on an interactive map with filters (species, park, district).
  • FR2: Show detailed tree information when clicking on a tree (species, age, park).
  • FR3: Provide statistics dashboards (species distribution, counts per park).
