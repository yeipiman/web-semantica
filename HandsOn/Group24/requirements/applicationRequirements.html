Application Requirements — “Urban Environmental Insights”

The application integrates three datasets: (1) tree inventory in green areas across districts of Madrid, (2) environmental protection expenditure by enterprises in Spain, and (3) nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) levels in urban areas. The objective is to provide an integrated view to analyze the relationship between urban greenery, pollution levels, and environmental investment.

Target Users

  • City planners and municipal technicians interested in urban sustainability.
  • Researchers and students analyzing urban ecology or environmental policy.
  • Citizens interested in air quality and urban green spaces.

User Stories

  • As a city planner, I want to visualize a map showing green areas and tree distributions across districts.
  • As a researcher, I want to filter by district and year to extract subsets of the data for analysis.
  • As a citizen, I want to compare NO₂ pollution trends with green coverage in different areas.

Functional Requirements

  • F1. Interactive map displaying tree counts per district, green areas, and air quality stations.
  • F2. Filtering panel by district, year, and environmental indicator.
  • F3. Time series graphs and key performance indicators (KPIs) for pollution, tree coverage, and environmental expenditure.
  • F4. Detailed profile pages for districts or green areas, showing trees, NO₂ levels, and expenditure data.
  • F5. CSV export of filtered datasets for further analysis.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • NF1. RDF publication linking to GeoNames, Wikidata, and OSM.
  • NF2. Open licensing (CC-BY 4.0) with proper attribution.
  • NF3. Performance acceptable for large datasets, including Madrid-wide tree inventory.
  • NF4. Accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA minimum).

Data and RDF Linking

  • Vocabs: DCAT, GeoSPARQL/WGS84, SOSA/SSN, QB.
  • Stable URIs for trees, green areas, districts, and pollution monitoring stations.
  • External links: GeoNames, Wikidata, NUTS/LAU.

Key Metrics

  • Number of trees per district and green area.
  • Distribution of tree species.
  • Average NO₂ levels per district and year.
  • Evolution of environmental protection expenditure over time.

Roadmap

  1. Data cleaning and normalization of CSVs.
  2. Preliminary publication and validation of RDF schemas.
  3. Transformation to RDF and linking with external vocabularies.
  4. Prototype of interactive dashboard and user testing.
